The Kerala Students Union (KSU) is the student wing of the Indian National Congress in Kerala. It is one of the oldest and most influential student organizations in the state, with a rich history of advocating for students' rights and social justice. KSU was founded in 1957 by M. A. John, a prominent figure in the Indian National Congress, with the aim of providing a platform for students to voice their concerns and contribute to the development of the state. Over the years, KSU has played a significant role in shaping the political and social landscape of Kerala, and its members have gone on to become prominent leaders in various fields. The organization is known for its commitment to secularism, democracy, and social justice, and it continues to be a leading voice for students across the state.
